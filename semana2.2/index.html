Astronomy

Astronomy is the science that studies celestial bodies in the universe, such as stars, planets, moons, comets, galaxies, and space in general, as well as the phenomena that occur outside of Earth. It seeks to understand how these bodies formed, how they move, what properties they have, and how they interact with each other.


Branches of Astronomy:

- Observational Astronomy:
studies the universe using telescope and satellites.

- Astrophysics:
combines physics and astronomy to understand the physical
properties of celestial bodies.

- Cosmology:
studies the universe as whole: origin, structure, and fate.

tools: description:
optical telescopes: to observe visible light from stars and planets.
Radio telescopes: To detect radio waves from space.
satellites and Space probes: Like the Hubble Telescope or Voyager.
Computer Simulations: To model galaxies, star formation and cosmic collisions.
